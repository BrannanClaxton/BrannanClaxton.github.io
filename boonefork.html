The Boone Fork trail is one of the most popular trails in Boone and, arguably, all of WNC. It is located off the Blue Ridge Parkway near Blowing Rock, and is a 5.2 mile loop trail. While this hike does not go to the top of a mountain or have sweeping, wide views, it is one of the most diverse and feature rich hikes in the area. This hike starts in a meadow, near a parking lot with toilets and picnic tables, and follows a stream downhill into a forest. The best part of the route is near the beginning, when the trail diverges from the stream, and you must take a steep, short side trail down to the bottom of what is known as Hebron Falls. This is a large, rocky slope that the stream flows down, and that can be climbed or bouldered to the top of the falls. From the top, you have a great view of the falls and the boulder formations. This is one of my favorite destinations in all of Boone, and if you only wanted to visit Hebron falls you could turn the hike into an out-and-back trail that would cut out a lot of distance. The rest of the route is mostly flat, and winds through a deep forest. While houses are visible at a few points, most of it is in the wilderness. The route ends through Julian Price campground, which offers camping and lake access.
